Larry Itliong

The Filipino immigrant who unapologetically fought for laborer rights.

Larry Itliong's life timeline:

  • October 25, 1913: Larry Dulay Itliong is born in San Nicolas, Pangasinan, Philippines.
  • 1929: At the age of 16, Itliong immigrates to the United States.
  • 1930s-1940s: Itliong becomes involved in various labor activities, working in different industries such as agriculture, canneries, and as a seasonal farm laborer.
  • 1950: Larry Itliong becomes a key figure in the labor movement when he helps organize a successful strike by Filipino farmworkers in asparagus fields in Stockton, California.
  • 1956: Itliong helps establish the Filipino Farm Labor Union in California.
  • 1965: Larry Itliong becomes the leader of the Agricultural Workers Organizing Committee (AWOC), a labor union primarily composed of Filipino farmworkers.
  • 1965-1966: AWOC, under Itliong's leadership, leads the Delano Grape Strike, a significant farm labor strike involving Filipino and Mexican farmworkers.
  • 1966: The National Farm Workers Association (NFWA), led by Cesar Chavez, joins forces with AWOC to form the United Farm Workers (UFW). Larry Itliong plays a crucial role in the merger.
  • 1971: Itliong resigns from the UFW due to disagreements with Chavez over the direction and leadership of the organization.
  • February 8, 1977: Larry Itliong passes away in Delano, California, at the age of 63.
